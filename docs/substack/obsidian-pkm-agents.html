You have an Obsidian vault and you want AI to work with it. You search for options and find hundreds of repos. Most are weekend projects. Here's what the data says about what actually works.

PT-Edge tracks 700+ repos across personal knowledge management, Obsidian plugins, MCP servers, and agent tools. We score them daily. The landscape splits into three layers.

Layer 1: AI-native PKM apps (the real tools)

khoj (79/100, 33K+ stars) is the quality leader. Self-hostable, works with any LLM, functions as a genuine AI second brain. If you want one tool and you're comfortable self-hosting, start here.

Smart Connections (4.7K stars) is the most adopted Obsidian-specific AI plugin. Semantic links as you write, AI chat that understands your vault, supports local models or 100+ APIs.

Note Companion (64/100) goes beyond chat — automatic file organisation, tag suggestions, and note processing. For vaults that have become a dumping ground.

For teams, SurfSense (13K+ stars, 894 commits last month) is an open-source NotebookLM alternative with extraordinary development velocity.

Layer 2: MCP bridge servers (the infrastructure play)

MCP servers give any AI agent structured access to your vault files. Your notes stay local; the model can be local or cloud.

mcpvault (946 stars) is the adoption leader — lightweight, safe, read-oriented. obsidian-mcp-server (396 stars) is more comprehensive: read, write, search, organise via MCP.

The architectural advantage: MCP separates the AI model from the data layer. Switch between Claude, a local Llama model, or any future LLM without rebuilding your integration.

Layer 3: Agent-native tools (the frontier)

obsidian-claude-pkm (1.2K stars) is the standout — a starter kit for Claude Code as a persistent vault teammate. iwe (60/100, 735 stars) is agent-first Markdown knowledge management in Rust.

The 33 repos in the obsidian-vault-agents category average just 23/100 quality. Most are experiments. But this is where the space is heading — AI agents that manage your knowledge autonomously.

The privacy decision

Obsidian users care about privacy. Fully local options: khoj (self-hostable), Smart2Brain, obsidian-sonar (completely offline). Cloud/API options: Note Companion and Smart Connections support both. MCP servers are inherently local — your notes are read on your machine.
