Wave 3: LLM + Evolution (The AlphaEvolve Wave) — Emerging Fast

Google DeepMind's AlphaEvolve announcement in May 2025 triggered a wave of open-source implementations that use LLMs as the mutation operator in evolutionary search. Instead of random mutations, the LLM proposes intelligent code modifications, and evolutionary selection keeps the best ones.

This wave is less than a year old. No repo has achieved dominance. The quality scores are low because these are early-stage projects — most lack packages, documentation, and community adoption. Where to Build

Across the evolutionary AI landscape, the quality gap is the recurring signal. Demand exists — the stars, downloads, and fork counts prove it. But the supply is either academically excellent and practically unmaintained (Waves 1-2), too new to evaluate (Wave 3), or backed by big companies who may or may not open-source sustainably (Wave 4).

The whitespace analysis points to three specific gaps:

  1. A modern evolutionary algorithm library that is actually maintained. Quality gap {{ cats['ml-frameworks:evolutionary-algorithm-frameworks'].quality_gap_score }}/100 in the classical frameworks category. gplearn and scikit-opt get real downloads but neither is actively developed. EvoTorch is the closest modern option but has not achieved dominance.
  2. A quality-scored comparison layer for the AlphaEvolve wave. Dozens of implementations exist with no way to evaluate which ones work. This is a review and benchmarking opportunity, not a build opportunity.
  3. Agent evolution infrastructure. The convergence of evolutionary computing and agentic AI is the most interesting space. The question is whether agent self-improvement becomes a platform capability or a standalone layer.
