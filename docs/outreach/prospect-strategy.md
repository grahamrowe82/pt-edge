# Prospect Strategy

Target buyer: AI-focused consultancies (5-50 people) who advise clients on tooling, architecture, or build-vs-buy decisions.

---

## Where to Find Prospects

### LinkedIn
- Search: "AI consulting" OR "AI advisory" OR "ML consulting"
- Filter: 5-50 employees, founded 2020+
- Look for: "AI strategy", "LLM implementation", "AI infrastructure consulting"
- Check if they post content about AI tools ÔÇö content producers need data

### Clutch.co / GoodFirms
- Category: Artificial Intelligence, Machine Learning
- Filter: small/mid-size, English-speaking markets
- Sort by reviews ÔÇö active firms with recent reviews are actively delivering

### AI Newsletter Authors
- People writing weekly/monthly AI ecosystem roundups
- Substack search: "AI tools", "AI landscape", "open source AI"
- These people are doing manually what PT-Edge automates

### Conference Speakers
- AI Engineer Summit, NeurIPS (industry track), AI DevWorld
- Look for talks on: tooling evaluation, infrastructure decisions, AI ops
- Speakers are public experts ÔÇö they need defensible data

### Twitter/X
- Accounts doing AI tool comparisons, "awesome-X" list maintainers
- People quote-tweeting GitHub release announcements
- Search: "which LLM framework", "comparing agent frameworks", "AI tools landscape"

---

## Qualifying Criteria

A good prospect hits 3+ of these:

| Criterion | Why It Matters |
|-----------|---------------|
| 5-50 people | Small enough to decide fast, big enough to pay |
| AI infrastructure focus (not pure research) | They evaluate tools, not just publish papers |
| Producing content (blog/newsletter/reports) | Content producers need fresh data ÔÇö they feel the pain |
| Serving enterprise clients | Enterprise engagements justify tooling spend |
| No existing systematic tooling | If they already use Pitchbook+Crunchbase they're less likely to add another tool |
| Active GitHub presence | Shows they live in the ecosystem, not just advise on it |

### Disqualifiers
- Pure academic/research labs (no budget, wrong use case)
- Large consultancies (Accenture, Deloitte) ÔÇö too slow, too many stakeholders
- Single-person freelancers ÔÇö unlikely to pay for API access

## Outreach Cadence

1. **Day 0:** Send initial email with trial key
2. **Day 5-7:** Follow-up with specific data point
3. **Day 14:** Final follow-up (different angle or offer a recorded demo)
4. **No reply after 3 touches:** Move to `passed`, revisit in 60 days

Aim for 5-10 outbound emails per week. Quality over volume ÔÇö each email should have a genuine personalization hook.
